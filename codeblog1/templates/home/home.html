Advocacy Tips
  • Start conversations with friends, teachers, and family about menstrual hygiene.
  • Organize a "Pads & Kindness" drive at your school or community center.
  • Use art, posters, or social media to spread awareness in a creative way.
  • Invite a health expert to talk to your class or club about menstrual health.
Safety & Precautions
  • Always get permission from a parent or guardian before sharing photos or personal stories.
  • Never share private information like phone numbers or addresses online.
  • Work with trusted adults (teachers, NGOs) when organizing events or drives.
  • Use safe, clean materials if making DIY pads, and wash your hands before and after.

Pads & Kindness Drive
How to Organize:
  • Set a date and location for the drive (e.g., school or community center).
  • Collect pads, tampons, and underwear from donors.
  • Distribute the items to girls in need, with kindness and respect.
  • Include educational materials about menstrual hygiene.
DIY Reusable Pad Workshop
How to Host:
  • Gather materials: fabric, scissors, thread, and sewing needles.
  • Invite girls and women to a workshop (with guardian permission).
  • Teach step-by-step how to make reusable pads.
  • Discuss hygiene and care for reusable pads.
Poster Contest for Awareness
How to Organize:
  • Set a theme (e.g., "Break the Silence on Menstruation").
  • Invite students to create posters with facts, myths, or positive messages.
  • Display the posters at school and award prizes for the best designs.
  • Use the posters to educate others about menstrual hygiene.
Teacher Training Day
How to Organize:
  • Invite a health expert or NGO to train teachers.
  • Cover topics like menstrual health, stigma, and how to support girls.
  • Provide resources for teachers to use in their classrooms.
  • Encourage open discussions and questions.
Short Research Survey
How to Conduct:
  • Create a simple survey with questions about access to pads, school facilities, and comfort levels.
  • Get permission from your school and guardians.
  • Collect responses anonymously and analyze the results.
  • Share findings with teachers, parents, and NGOs to advocate for change.
